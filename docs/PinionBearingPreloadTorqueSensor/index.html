During end 2012 / spring 2013, when driving  with my VW Bus T3,   a  blustering noise could be heared clearly, coming out of the front transmission of the 4WD T3 Syncro. It was clear that a general overhaul of the front transmission had to be done. Thanks to the intensive help of two friends, mainly concerning the physically straining parts of the work which I could never do with my affected backbone, it was so far possible to do this work on my own. Repairing a transmission means mainly having available a lot of special tools. A lot of them I could already find in  my collection. Then, some of the missing tools I could buy, but many of them I had to create myself because they are hardly available. A long time I was looking for a matching torque meter whose range and construction allows to measure the preload torque of pinion bearings.

One day by chance, I found a second hand high precision Lebow torque sensor in the range of +/- 600 Ncm - exactly the range needed for our purpose. So I decided to create also this special tool on my own. The idea of the construction is to have a hand-tool part consisting of torquesensor plus electronics installed on a crank handle where one can turn around the pinion bearings built in the transmission. This hand tool sends the sensor values via rs232-radio transmission to the display placed aside in the field of sight of the operator. The hand tool electronics-processor, an ATMega328, was programmed using AVR Studio 5.1 and the display part was developed using QNX Momentics IDE.
